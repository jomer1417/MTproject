TOURIST SPOT OF SAN NICOLAS

OLD PLAZA

The Old Plaza of San Nicolas, Ilocos Norte is a historic public square that traces its roots to the Spanish colonial era, serving as the heart of the town where civic, religious, and cultural activities took place. Surrounded by important landmarks such as the San Nicolas de Tolentino Church, the municipal hall, and heritage houses including the Balay San Nicolas (the oldest and most prominent ancestral house in the province), the plaza reflects the traditional layout of Ilocano towns influenced by Spanish urban planning. Over time, it has witnessed the community's history - from colonial governance to wartime events - and continues to be a gathering place for festivals like the Damili Festival. Today, the Old Plaza and its surrounding structures stand not only as a reminder of the town's rich past but also as a vibrant cultural hub that preserves Ilocano identity and traditions for future generations.

Balay San Nicolas

Balay San Nicolas is a heritage-inspired building located in San Nicolas, Ilocos Norte. It serves as a cultural hub that showcases the town's rich history, traditions, and artistry. Designed with Spanish and Ilocano architectural influences, the Balay reflects the charm of ancestral houses through its wooden details, capiz windows, and classic brickwork. Today, it stands as a venue for community gatherings, exhibits, and cultural events, giving visitors a glimpse into the lifestyle and heritage of the Ilocano people.

San Nicolas de Tolentino Parish Church

San Nicolas de Tolentino Parish Church is a historic Catholic church built during the Spanish colonial period. It is one of the oldest churches in Ilocos Norte, showcasing traditional Baroque-inspired architecture with thick brick walls, a sturdy bell tower, and intricate details that reflect both Spanish and Ilocano artistry. Dedicated to St. Nicholas of Tolentino, the church serves as a spiritual and cultural landmark of the town. It has witnessed centuries of faith, tradition, and community life, making it not only a place of worship but also a treasured heritage site for locals and visitors alike.

Pottery Shops located at Barangay Barabar San Nicolas

The Damili Industry of San Nicolas, Ilocos Norte is one of the town's most treasured traditions, known for producing handcrafted clay jars, pots, stoves, and other earthenware. "Damili," the Ilocano term for pottery, is a skill passed down through generations, reflecting both artistry and practicality in Ilocano life. In Barangay Barabar, many families continue this craft, keeping the tradition alive.

One of the notable potters is Paulina Rangcapan, a master pottery maker who has spent decades shaping clay into beautiful and functional pieces. Her work represents not only livelihood but also cultural pride, as each creation carries the mark of Ilocano heritage. Through artisans like her, San Nicolas remains the Pottery Capital of Ilocos Norte, drawing visitors who wish to witness and experience the timeless art of damili.
