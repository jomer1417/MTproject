DAMILI FESTIVAL

In San Nicolas, Ilocos Norte, December is a month of thanksgiving for all the blessings received throughout the year, celebrated through the Damili Festival. This festival lasts for a month and includes dances, parades, shows, beauty contests, trade fairs, exhibits, and sports events. The highlight happens in the last week of December, with the Dance Pageantry, a street dance competition that shows the town's traditional craft - damili or pottery.

The festival is a way for the people to honor and preserve this old tradition. The main product, the banga or clay pot, has become a symbol of Ilocano heritage, passed on through generations. The Dance Pageantry presents the different stages of pottery-making, from gathering clay to firing the pots, set to the Ilocano song Agdamdamili. This performance reflects the Ilocano values of humility, hard work, and love for life. All barangays join the street dance, wearing colorful costumes inspired by old times and using props like clay pots, farming tools, and animals.

TOURIST SPOT OF SAN NICOLAS

OLD PLAZA

The Old Plaza of San Nicolas, Ilocos Norte is a historic public square that traces its roots to the Spanish colonial era, serving as the heart of the town where civic, religious, and cultural activities took place. Surrounded by important landmarks such as the San Nicolas de Tolentino Church, the municipal hall, and heritage houses including the Balay San Nicolas (the oldest and most prominent ancestral house in the province), the plaza reflects the traditional layout of Ilocano towns influenced by Spanish urban planning. Over time, it has witnessed the community's history - from colonial governance to wartime events - and continues to be a gathering place for festivals like the Damili Festival. Today, the Old Plaza and its surrounding structures stand not only as a reminder of the town's rich past but also as a vibrant cultural hub that preserves Ilocano identity and traditions for future generations.

Balay San Nicolas

Balay San Nicolas is a heritage-inspired building located in San Nicolas, Ilocos Norte. It serves as a cultural hub that showcases the town's rich history, traditions, and artistry. Designed with Spanish and Ilocano architectural influences, the Balay reflects the charm of ancestral houses through its wooden details, capiz windows, and classic brickwork. Today, it stands as a venue for community gatherings, exhibits, and cultural events, giving visitors a glimpse into the lifestyle and heritage of the Ilocano people.

San Nicolas de Tolentino Parish Church

San Nicolas de Tolentino Parish Church is a historic Catholic church built during the Spanish colonial period. It is one of the oldest churches in Ilocos Norte, showcasing traditional Baroque-inspired architecture with thick brick walls, a sturdy bell tower, and intricate details that reflect both Spanish and Ilocano artistry. Dedicated to St. Nicholas of Tolentino, the church serves as a spiritual and cultural landmark of the town. It has witnessed centuries of faith, tradition, and community life, making it not only a place of worship but also a treasured heritage site for locals and visitors alike.

Pottery Shops located at Barangay Barabar San Nicolas

The Damili Industry of San Nicolas, Ilocos Norte is one of the town's most treasured traditions, known for producing handcrafted clay jars, pots, stoves, and other earthenware. "Damili," the Ilocano term for pottery, is a skill passed down through generations, reflecting both artistry and practicality in Ilocano life. In Barangay Barabar, many families continue this craft, keeping the tradition alive.

One of the notable potters is Paulina Rangcapan, a master pottery maker who has spent decades shaping clay into beautiful and functional pieces. Her work represents not only livelihood but also cultural pride, as each creation carries the mark of Ilocano heritage. Through artisans like her, San Nicolas remains the Pottery Capital of Ilocos Norte, drawing visitors who wish to witness and experience the timeless art of damili.

History of San Nicolas

Like any other town in the then singular province of Ilocos, San Nicolas was established by Augustinian missionaries. Vigan, the first Spanish mission center in this region, was founded in 1572. In the year 1575, when the Spaniards first explored the Laoag (Padsan) River, they met hostile resistance from the natives of San Nicolas and Laoag. In another expedition dispatched from Vigan in 1584 San Nicolas was established and Laoag followed a year later.

San Nicolas Catholic Church built in 1701 is one of the oldest buildings in this province. Its original structure is one of the must see places of the town. The Church and the attached Santa Rosa Academy Building, the well preserved Municipal Hall and the old Town Plaza sprawled between them constitute the heart of the town center. These buildings and structures are originally built and continue to be festooned with earthen bricks, which is among the products of the surviving locals.

Products of San Nicolas

TAGAPULOT

In the coastal town of San Nicolas, Ilocos Norte, Philippines, locals craft "Tagapulot" a cherished traditional sweet by simmering fresh sugarcane juice in large, open pans over wood fires until it thickens into a glossy, sticky confection with a rich, caramelized flavor. This time-honored delicacy not only provides a vital source of income for families but also preserves cultural heritage, drawing visitors and sustaining community traditions.

SUKA AND BASI

Suka and Basi of San Nicolas, Ilocos Norte San Nicolas is known for its rich traditions and local products, including Suka and Basi, both made from sugarcane. Suka, or vinegar, is naturally fermented to produce a sour and slightly sweet flavor, perfect for Ilocano dishes. Basi, meanwhile, is a traditional sugarcane wine that has been enjoyed for generations. It has a smooth, sweet, and tangy taste that represents Ilocano heritage. These two products showcase the town's creativity, agricultural skills, and dedication to preserving local culture.
